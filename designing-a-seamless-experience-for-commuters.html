Reading on the Go: Designing a Seamless Experience for Commuters

Background

When we’re reading on the go especially in public transport, we often get interrupted by events such as the bus arriving at a stop or social interactions with other people. Many interesting articles get abandoned or added to the ever growing “read later” list.

Identifying the problem

There are a lot of challenges people often face when reading on the go. I did some personal research and reached out to some of my friends about their experience in situations like this.I was able to highlight a few which include:
  • Interruptions: Readers can get interrupted by events such as the bus arriving at a stop or social interactions with other people, leading to a lack of focus and retention.
  • Distractions: The environment can be noisy and distracting, making it difficult for readers to concentrate.
  • Screen Glare: The screen glare from the sun or overhead lights can be a major distraction and strain on the eyes.
  • Screen Size: The limited screen size of mobile devices can be a challenge, especially when reading longer articles.
  • Uncomfortable Reading Positions: The physical discomfort from holding the device in an awkward position for an extended period can cause fatigue and strain on the eyes and neck.

The objective

Identifying specific goals as metrics for success is crucial for achieving success for an product.
For a probable solution, the user should at least be able to carry out these tasks successfully which would help improve their reading experience on the go.
  • User being able to mark where they stopped reading in the event of interruptions.
  • Users being able to continue reading after said distractions are gone.
  • Improve overall reading experience and interactions.

How can this be solved?

From my conversations with friends and the research I did, I was able to identify ways in which these interruptions could be overcome and retain the optimal reading experience. These include:
  • Article Summaries: AI has proven to be a very useful tool in these modern times. A feature that summarizes articles in a concise format will make it easier for readers to quickly catch up with the content.
  • Audio Mode: A feature that allows users to listen to articles instead of reading, reducing eye strain and allowing for multitasking.
  • Offline Mode: A feature that allows users to download articles for offline reading, eliminating the need for a stable internet connection.
  • Personalized Reading Preferences/Settings: A feature that allows users to customize the reading experience, including font size, background color, and brightness to match environmental ambience.
  • Resume Reading (Remind for later): A feature that automatically saves the reader's place and allows them to resume reading from where they left off and also sends notifications after a stipulated time to do so.

The audience

Given the general demography, this solution would mostly cater to the younger generation who go out in groups. They’re is often tech-savvy and comfortable using apps and other digital tools to simplify their lives.

Learnings and key takeaways

Working on this project was a great learning experience for me. As a designer, I was faced with many challenges and obstacles that required me to draw on my skills and experiences to come up with creative solutions.
One of the biggest challenges was the need to create a user-friendly and intuitive interface that would make it easy for users to manage their cards and access their financial information. To overcome this challenge, I relied on my deep knowledge of user-centered design and my experience creating similar products in the past. This allowed me to create a platform that was both functional and easy to use.
I learned a lot from this project, and I am confident that it will help me to continue growing and improving as a designer.
